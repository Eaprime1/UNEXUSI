## What Is GUBERNACULUM?

**GUBERNACULUM** is the governance and steering repository ‚Äî where the ecosystem's decision-making principles, operational rules, and directional frameworks live.

In embryology, the gubernaculum is the structure that guides (not forces) the position of developing organs. It steers through constraint and ligament, not muscle. **GUBERNACULUM in this ecosystem works the same way:** it doesn't push ‚Äî it provides the tethers and constraints within which the system finds its own direction.

**Prime 7 ‚Äî Ship:** Seven is the completion prime, the distribution prime. Governance is what allows things to complete and ship ‚Äî without it, everything is perpetual build. GUBERNACULUM enables the "ship" state.

## Visionary Adjustments

> *"We need a dress up section, the unofficial reviews (tracked), Visionary adjustments."*

**Visionary Adjustments** are changes that come from the highest level of the system ‚Äî decisions that override or redirect the normal flow because the Visionary sees something the system doesn't yet.

These are **not errors** and **not exceptions** ‚Äî they are a formal part of the governance process:

1. The adjustment is made
2. It is logged in `GUBERNACULUM/log/DECISIONS.md`
3. The reasoning is captured (even if brief)
4. The adjusted state becomes the new baseline

Without this formal layer, Visionary adjustments are invisible ‚Äî the system can't learn from them and future sessions lose the context. With it, they become part of the chain of custody.

## The Dress Up Section

Before content moves from a domain repo to NEXUSIAM ‚Üí HODIE, it passes through **Dress Up**:

1. **Formatting** ‚Äî consistent markdown, correct headers, proper prime labels
2. **Voice check** ‚Äî does this sound like the character/concept it represents?
3. **Prime alignment** ‚Äî is the prime frequency consistent throughout?
4. **Link check** ‚Äî do all cross-repo references resolve?
5. **Chain of custody** ‚Äî is the filing complete?

Dress Up is run in `ASHKHARH` for character content, in `TIEZERK` for concept content, and in `GUBERNACULUM` for governance content.
